Question Resources

Welcome to the questions page! This faith is a faith of open and encouraged learning. There are many tools available to us to learn the mysteries of God so far as we are able. Austin Farrer once stated:

“Though argument does not create conviction, the lack of it destroys belief. What seems to be proved may not be embraced; but what no one shows the ability to defend is quickly abandoned. Rational argument does not create belief, but it maintains a climate in which belief can flourish.”

(Austin Farrer, “The Christian Apologist,” in Light on C. S. Lewis, ed. Jocelyn Gibb (New York: Harcourt, Brace, and World, 1965), 26.)

Some products of a developed theology with a complex history are the questions we have because of it. These can apply to any realm of topics in the Church, be it theological, historical, cultural, or otherwise. A lot of these questions are hard to answer completely because we don't have all of the answers. Fortunately, most of these questions have all been tackled before by scholars and productive readers, even writing extensively on them at a scholarly level. Although, some arguments are naturally better than others, and we learn a lot with time. Many websites are kept up for this purpose as new information comes out, addressing these problems directly with full transparency on the problems. On this page you can find a list of websites and resources categorized by topic in order to find the best place and the most qualified people to answer your tough questions.

Book of Mormon Central:

This is the perfect website for answering just about any question regarding the text of the Book of Mormon. It has short but detailed videos, and also various resources for Come Follow Me and related questions.

FAIR:

An acronym for "Faithful Answers, Informed Response." It does just that, giving very detailed and specific answers to the widest range of questions. It cites sources and lays out the best arguments for your toughest questions.

Mormonr:

A resource similar to FAIR, funded by the B. H. Roberts Foundation. This question-answering resource is incredibly direct and to the point, documenting precisely sources and opinions on each topic. They also make very funny memes!
